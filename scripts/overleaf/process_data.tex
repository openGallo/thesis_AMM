\begin{abstract}
This document describes the data processing pipeline for the AMM thesis
project. It records which Python script transforms each raw dataset,
the exact formulas used to derive key variables, the output file formats
and column-level data dictionaries, and the dependency order between
processing steps. The processing layer operates entirely on files
produced by the import scripts (\texttt{run\_cex.py} and
\texttt{run\_dex.py}) and writes its outputs to
\texttt{data\_processed/}. No raw files are modified.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Important notes on accuracy.}
Every column description, formula, constant, and dependency in this
document has been verified directly against the source scripts
(\texttt{process\_cex\_price.py},
\texttt{process\_cex\_orderbook.py},
\texttt{process\_dex\_pool\_hourly.py},
\texttt{process\_dex\_swaps.py},
\texttt{process\_dex\_lp\_positions.py},
\texttt{process\_dex\_lvr.py},
\texttt{process\_merged\_panel.py},
\texttt{process\_calibration.py},
\texttt{run\_all.py}).
\end{abstract}

\section{Abbreviations and Terminology}
\label{sec:glossary}

\begin{xltabular}{\linewidth}{L{3.0cm}Y}
\toprule
\textbf{Term} & \textbf{Definition} \\
\midrule
GBM & Geometric Brownian Motion. The price process $dS = \mu S\,dt +
      \sigma S\,dW$ used to model ETH price dynamics. \\
LVR & Loss-Versus-Rebalancing. A theoretical bound on the cost to a
      liquidity provider of providing liquidity in an AMM, measured
      against a passive rebalancing strategy. \\
TVL & Total value locked. USD value of tokens held in the pool. \\
OHLC & Open, High, Low, Close. Standard candlestick price fields. \\
Basis & Price difference between DEX and CEX, expressed in basis points:
        $(p^{\mathrm{DEX}} - p^{\mathrm{CEX}}) / p^{\mathrm{CEX}} \times
        10{,}000$. \\
Arb flag & Arbitrage flag. Set when $|\text{basis}| > 5$ bp, i.e.\ when
           the DEX price deviates from the CEX by more than the pool's
           round-trip fee. \\
Lognormal fit & Maximum-likelihood fit to $\log |q_i| \sim
                \mathcal{N}(\mu_q, \sigma_q^2)$ for trade sizes $q_i > 0$. \\
AR(1) & First-order autoregressive model: $y_t = \rho y_{t-1} + \epsilon_t$.
        Applied here to log gas price. \\
Stress regime & Hours where the 24h rolling realized vol exceeds the 90th
                percentile of the full sample. \\
sqrtPriceX96 & The pool square-root price as a Q64.96 fixed-point integer.
               True USDC/WETH price: $10^{12} / (\texttt{sqrtPriceX96} /
               2^{96})^2$. \\
Synthetic price & $p^{\mathrm{ETH/USDC}}_{\mathrm{syn}} =
                  p^{\mathrm{ETH/USDT}} / p^{\mathrm{USDC/USDT}}$.
                  Used to fill ETHUSDC gaps before August 2022. \\
\bottomrule
\end{xltabular}

\section{CEX Price Panel: \texttt{process\_cex\_price.py}}
\label{sec:cexprice}

\subsection{Inputs}

\begin{lstlisting}
data_raw/CEX/binance/spot/daily/klines/{SYMBOL}/1m/*.csv
data_raw/CEX/binance/spot/monthly/klines/{SYMBOL}/1m/*.csv
\end{lstlisting}

Symbols loaded: \texttt{ETHUSDC}, \texttt{ETHUSDT}, \texttt{USDCUSDT}.
Daily and monthly archives are combined automatically; duplicates on
\texttt{open\_time\_ms} are dropped before merging.

\subsection{Key Logic}

\paragraph{Microsecond detection.}
Binance monthly klines distributed from 2025 onward use microsecond
timestamps instead of milliseconds. Rows where
$\texttt{open\_time\_ms} > 10^{15}$ are identified as microsecond-origin
and divided by $10^{3}$ before any other conversion.

\paragraph{Synthetic ETH/USDC price.}
ETHUSDC was not listed on Binance before approximately August 2022.
For earlier periods:
\[
  p^{\mathrm{ETH/USDC}}_{\mathrm{syn},t}
  = \frac{p^{\mathrm{ETH/USDT}}_t}{p^{\mathrm{USDC/USDT}}_t}.
\]
The primary price series \texttt{eth\_usdc\_price} uses the direct
ETHUSDC close when available and falls back to the synthetic when not.

\paragraph{Realized volatility (annualized).}
Log returns are computed on \texttt{eth\_usdc\_price}:
\[
  r_t = \log p^{\mathrm{ETH/USDC}}_t - \log p^{\mathrm{ETH/USDC}}_{t-1}.
\]
Rolling realized volatility is annualized by the square root of the number
of 1-minute bars per year ($\sqrt{365.25 \times 24 \times 60}$):

\begin{align*}
  \hat{\sigma}^{(1\mathrm{h})}_{\mathrm{ann},t}  &=
    \sqrt{365.25 \times 24 \times 60}\;
    \mathrm{sd}(r_{t-59},\ldots,r_t),
    \quad \text{min.\ 30 obs.}, \\
  \hat{\sigma}^{(24\mathrm{h})}_{\mathrm{ann},t} &=
    \sqrt{365.25 \times 24 \times 60}\;
    \mathrm{sd}(r_{t-1439},\ldots,r_t),
    \quad \text{min.\ 720 obs.}, \\
  \hat{\sigma}^{(7\mathrm{d})}_{\mathrm{ann},t}  &=
    \sqrt{365.25 \times 24 \times 60}\;
    \mathrm{sd}(r_{t-10079},\ldots,r_t),
    \quad \text{min.\ 3600 obs.}
\end{align*}

Volatility columns carry the \texttt{\_ann} suffix throughout all files.

\paragraph{Hourly resampling.}
The 1-minute panel is resampled to hourly frequency using
\texttt{pandas.resample("1h").ohlc()} on the price series.
Realized vol columns are forward-sampled with \texttt{.last()}.
Volume and trade-count are summed over the hour.

\subsection{Outputs}

\begin{lstlisting}
data_processed/CEX/cex_price_1m.csv       (2,276,560 rows, ~353 MB)
data_processed/CEX/cex_price_hourly.csv   (37,944 rows, ~5.5 MB)
\end{lstlisting}

\section{DEX Pool Hourly/Daily: \texttt{process\_dex\_pool\_hourly.py}}
\label{sec:dexpool}

\subsection{Inputs}

\begin{lstlisting}
data_raw/DEX/pool_hour_data.csv
data_raw/DEX/pool_day_data.csv
\end{lstlisting}

Produced by \texttt{fetch\_uniswap\_pool\_timeseries.py} (or the
\texttt{reconstruct\_pool\_timeseries\_from\_swaps.py} variant). The
\texttt{token0\_price} column in these files holds the
$\sqrt{P_{\mathrm{X96}}}$-derived ETH price (accurate on-chain price),
not the stale subgraph cached value. This distinction is critical: all
downstream price calculations use the X96-derived value.

\subsection{Key Derived Variables}

\paragraph{Price columns.}
\texttt{token0\_price} is renamed to \texttt{eth\_usdc\_price}.
The OHLC columns (\texttt{open}, \texttt{high}, \texttt{low},
\texttt{close}) are renamed to \texttt{eth\_usdc\_open},
\texttt{eth\_usdc\_high}, \texttt{eth\_usdc\_low},
\texttt{eth\_usdc\_close}.

\paragraph{Fee APR (annualized).}
\[
  \texttt{fee\_apr\_ann} =
  \begin{cases}
    \dfrac{\texttt{fees\_usd}}{\texttt{tvl\_usd}} \times 24 \times 365.25
    & \text{(hourly)}, \\[6pt]
    \dfrac{\texttt{fees\_usd}}{\texttt{tvl\_usd}} \times 365.25
    & \text{(daily)}.
  \end{cases}
\]
Zero TVL is replaced by NaN before division to avoid infinite values.

\paragraph{Volume over TVL.}
\[
  \texttt{vol\_over\_tvl} = \frac{\texttt{volume\_usd}}{\texttt{tvl\_usd}}.
\]

\paragraph{Hourly log return.}
\[
  \texttt{log\_return\_1h} = \log\!\left(\frac{\texttt{eth\_usdc\_close}_h}
  {\texttt{eth\_usdc\_close}_{h-1}}\right).
\]

\paragraph{Fee rate.}
\texttt{fee\_rate} $= 0.0005$ (the 0.05\% pool tier) is added as a
constant column to every row.

\subsection{Outputs}

\begin{lstlisting}
data_processed/DEX/dex_pool_hourly.csv   (43,728 rows, ~16.8 MB)
data_processed/DEX/dex_pool_daily.csv    (1,822 rows, ~0.7 MB)
\end{lstlisting}

\subsection{Key Columns: \texttt{dex\_pool\_hourly.csv}}

Index: \texttt{timestamp\_utc} (UTC datetime, hourly frequency,
2021-05-05 to 2026-04-30).

\midrule

\texttt{eth\_usdc\_open} & float/NaN & Hourly opening price. \\

\section{DEX Swap Panel: \texttt{process\_dex\_swaps.py}}
\label{sec:dexswaps}

\subsection{Inputs}

\begin{lstlisting}
data_raw/DEX/swaps_YYYY_MM.csv   (all months, 2021-05 to 2026-04)
\end{lstlisting}

All monthly files are concatenated. The combined DataFrame is sorted by
(\texttt{block\_number}, \texttt{log\_index}) to reproduce the exact
on-chain execution order within blocks, since all swaps within a block
share the same timestamp and timestamp alone is insufficient for ordering.

\subsection{Key Derived Variables}

\paragraph{On-chain ETH/USDC price (primary).}

The primary price column is derived from the raw
\texttt{sqrtPriceX96} field:
\[
  p^{\mathrm{USDC/WETH}} = \frac{10^{12}}{(\texttt{sqrtPriceX96} /
  2^{96})^2},
\]
where $10^{12} = 10^{18-6}$ corrects for the 12-decimal difference
between WETH (18 decimals) and USDC (6 decimals). This formula is
verified to machine precision across all 10.9M swaps (maximum relative
error $2.5 \times 10^{-16}$). The result is stored as
\texttt{eth\_usdc\_price\_x96} and aliased to \texttt{eth\_usdc\_price}.
The subgraph-cached \texttt{pool\_token0\_price} is retained as
\texttt{eth\_usdc\_price\_subgraph} for diagnostic comparison only.

\paragraph{Trade direction.}
\[
  \texttt{direction} = \begin{cases}
    \texttt{"buy\_eth"}  & \text{if } \texttt{buy\_eth} = \mathrm{True}, \\
    \texttt{"sell\_eth"} & \text{if } \texttt{buy\_eth} = \mathrm{False}.
  \end{cases}
\]

\paragraph{Gas cost in USD.}
\[
  \texttt{gas\_cost\_usd} = \texttt{gas\_cost\_eth} \times
  \texttt{eth\_usdc\_price}.
\]

\paragraph{Log price change.}
The log price change between consecutive swaps is computed from the
Q96-fixed-point encoding:
\[
  \Delta\log p_n = -2(\log s_n - \log s_{n-1}),
  \quad s = \texttt{sqrtPriceX96}.
\]
This is numerically exact for Uniswap v3's Q96 encoding. For cross-block
consecutive pairs the value is set to \texttt{NaN}: the price moved due
to external factors unrelated to the swap, and the quantity is not
interpretable as the swap's own price impact.

\paragraph{Trade size bucket.}
\[
  \texttt{trade\_size\_bucket} = \begin{cases}
    \texttt{"<1k"}      & |\texttt{amount\_usd}| \in (0,\,1{,}000], \\
    \texttt{"1k-10k"}   & |\texttt{amount\_usd}| \in (1{,}000,\,10{,}000], \\
    \texttt{"10k-100k"} & |\texttt{amount\_usd}| \in (10{,}000,\,100{,}000], \\
    \texttt{">100k"}    & |\texttt{amount\_usd}| > 100{,}000.
  \end{cases}
\]

\subsection{Output}

\begin{lstlisting}

\end{lstlisting}

\section{DEX LP Positions: \texttt{process\_dex\_lp\_positions.py}}
\label{sec:dexlp}

\subsection{Inputs}

\begin{lstlisting}

data_raw/DEX/burns_YYYY_MM.csv      (all months)
data_raw/DEX/collects_YYYY_MM.csv   (all months)
data_raw/DEX/positions_current.csv  (optional -- adds is_active flag)
\end{lstlisting}

\subsection{Position Key and Aggregation}

The position key is the triple $(\texttt{owner},\,\texttt{tick\_lower},\,
\texttt{tick\_upper})$. This is a pool-level key: if the owner is the
NonfungiblePositionManager contract, all NFT-wrapped positions at the
same range are merged. This is acceptable for aggregate analysis of
the 0.05\% pool; exact per-NFT accounting requires the NFT token ID
as the key.

Each event type is aggregated by sum over the key:
\begin{align*}
  \texttt{total\_minted\_usd}    &= \textstyle\sum \texttt{amount\_usd}
    \text{ (mints)}, \\
  \texttt{total\_burned\_usd}    &= \textstyle\sum \texttt{amount\_usd}
    \text{ (burns)}, \\
  \texttt{total\_collected\_usd} &= \textstyle\sum \texttt{amount\_usd}
    \text{ (collects)}.
\end{align*}
The three tables are outer-joined on the position key; positions appearing
in any event type are retained.

\subsection{Key Derived Variables}

\paragraph{Net P\&L.}
\[
  \texttt{net\_pnl\_usd} =
  \texttt{total\_burned\_usd} + \texttt{total\_collected\_usd}
  - \texttt{total\_minted\_usd}.
\]

\paragraph{Approximate fee income.}
In Uniswap v3 the \texttt{collect} call transfers both accumulated fees
\emph{and} any remaining principal from a prior burn. Therefore:
\[
  \texttt{fee\_income\_usd} \approx
  \texttt{total\_collected\_usd} - \texttt{total\_burned\_usd}.
\]
This approximation holds when each burn is followed promptly by a
collect. When the collects dataset is absent, \texttt{fee\_income\_usd}
is set to \texttt{NaN} rather than a spurious negative value.

\paragraph{Range width.}
\[
  \texttt{range\_width\_ticks} = \texttt{tick\_upper} - \texttt{tick\_lower}.
\]
\[
  \texttt{range\_width\_pct} = \min\!\Big(
  100 \times \big(1.0001^{\texttt{range\_width\_ticks}} - 1\big),\;
  500\%\Big).
\]
Log arithmetic is used to avoid floating-point overflow for extreme tick
ranges (the full Uniswap v3 range spans 1{,}774{,}544 ticks).

\paragraph{Position type.}
\[
  \texttt{position\_type} = \begin{cases}
    \texttt{"narrow"} & \texttt{range\_width\_pct} \leq 5\%, \\
    \texttt{"medium"} & 5\% < \texttt{range\_width\_pct} \leq 20\%, \\
    \texttt{"wide"}   & \texttt{range\_width\_pct} > 20\%.
  \end{cases}
\]

\paragraph{Duration.}
\[
  \texttt{duration\_days} = \frac{\texttt{last\_event\_utc} -
  \texttt{first\_mint\_utc}}{86{,}400\;\mathrm{s}},
\]
where \texttt{last\_event\_utc} is the maximum of \texttt{last\_burn\_utc}
and \texttt{last\_collect\_utc}.

\paragraph{Active flag.}
Positions with \texttt{liquidity} $\neq$ \texttt{"0"} in
\texttt{positions\_current.csv} are marked \texttt{is\_active = True}.
Positions absent from or with zero liquidity in that file receive
\texttt{False}. If \texttt{positions\_current.csv} is missing,
\texttt{is\_active = NaN}.

\section{DEX LVR: \texttt{process\_dex\_lvr.py}}
\label{sec:lvr}

\subsection{Theory}

Following Milionis, Moallemi, Roughgarden, and Zhang (2024):
\[
  d\mathrm{LVR}_t = \tfrac{1}{2} \sigma_t^2\, p_t^2\, |x'(p_t)|\, dt.
\]
For a Uniswap v3 active-range approximation with virtual liquidity $L$:
\[
  x(p) = L / \sqrt{p} \;\Rightarrow\; |x'(p)| = L / (2 p^{3/2}),
  \quad\Rightarrow\quad
  d\mathrm{LVR}_t = \frac{\sigma_t^2 L_t \sqrt{p_t}}{4}\, dt.
\]
Under the balanced-pool TVL approximation
($x_t \cdot p_t \approx \mathrm{TVL}_t / 2$):
\[
  \boxed{d\mathrm{LVR}_t \approx \frac{\mathrm{TVL}_t}{8}\,\sigma_t^2\, dt.}
\]

\subsection{Inputs}

\begin{lstlisting}
data_processed/DEX/dex_pool_hourly.csv
data_processed/CEX/cex_price_hourly.csv
\end{lstlisting}

The two files are inner-joined on their UTC index. Rows with missing TVL
or realized volatility are dropped.

\subsection{Key Derived Variables}

Let $\sigma_t^2 =
(\texttt{realized\_vol\_24h\_ann}_t)^2$ be the annualized variance
and $\Delta t = 1/8760$ years (one hour).

\paragraph{LVR rate (annualized fraction of TVL).}
\[
  \texttt{lvr\_rate\_ann} = \sigma^2_t / 8.
\]

\paragraph{LVR in USD per hour (TVL approximation).}
\[
  \texttt{lvr\_usd\_tvl\_approx} =
  \frac{\mathrm{TVL}_t}{8} \times \sigma^2_t \times \Delta t.
\]

\paragraph{LVR rate (v3 active-liquidity formula).}
When raw pool liquidity $L_t$ is available:
\[
  \texttt{lvr\_rate\_ann\_v3\_approx} =
  \frac{\sigma^2_t}{4} \times
  \frac{L_t \sqrt{p_t}}{\mathrm{TVL}_t}.
\]
The factor $L_t \sqrt{p_t}$ is in mixed units (raw liquidity $\times$
sqrt price); it is normalised by TVL to give a dimensionless rate for
comparability with the TVL approximation.

\paragraph{LVR to fee income ratio.}
\[
  \texttt{lvr\_to\_fee\_ratio} = \frac{\texttt{lvr\_usd\_tvl\_approx}}
  {\texttt{fees\_usd}},
\]
set to \texttt{NaN} when hourly fees are zero or negative.

\section{Calibration Parameters: \texttt{process\_calibration.py}}
\label{sec:calib}

\subsection{Purpose}

This script reads all processed files and extracts the reduced-form
parameter values needed to initialise the AMM simulation. It produces
a structured JSON file and a flat CSV for quick inspection.

\subsection{Inputs}

\begin{lstlisting}

data_processed/DEX/dex_swaps.csv
data_processed/DEX/dex_lvr_hourly.csv   (optional)
data_processed/CEX/cex_price_hourly.csv
\end{lstlisting}

Missing files generate a warning but do not abort the script; the
corresponding JSON section is simply omitted.

\subsection{Calibration Sections and Formulas}

\paragraph{Price dynamics (from \texttt{cex\_price\_hourly.csv}).}

\begin{align*}
  \hat{\sigma}_{\mathrm{hourly}} &= \mathrm{sd}(\texttt{log\_return\_1h}), \\
  \hat{\sigma}_{\mathrm{daily}}  &= \hat{\sigma}_{\mathrm{hourly}}
    \times \sqrt{24}, \\
  \hat{\mu}_{\mathrm{daily}}     &= \overline{\texttt{log\_return\_1h}}
    \times 24.
\end{align*}

This is the standard annualization convention for the thesis:
\[
  \sigma_{\mathrm{hourly}}
  \xrightarrow{\;\times\sqrt{24}\;}
  \sigma_{\mathrm{daily}}
  \xrightarrow{\;\times\sqrt{365.25}\;}
  \sigma_{\mathrm{ann}}.
\]
The calibration stores $\sigma_{\mathrm{daily}}$ and
$\sigma_{\mathrm{hourly}}$; the annualized form is derived externally
when needed.

\paragraph{Stress regime.}

The stress volatility threshold is defined as the 90th percentile of
$\texttt{realized\_vol\_24h\_ann}$ over the full sample:
\[
  \sigma^{*}_{\mathrm{stress}} =
  Q_{90}\!\left(\hat{\sigma}^{(24\mathrm{h})}_{\mathrm{ann}}\right).
\]
The stress fraction is the proportion of hours above this threshold.

\paragraph{Pool state (from \texttt{dex\_pool\_hourly.csv}).}

Pool state parameters are estimated over the full 2021-05 to 2026-04
period. The Poisson trade arrival intensity is approximated as:
\[
  \hat{\lambda}_{\mathrm{hour}} = \overline{\texttt{tx\_count}}
  \quad \text{(mean hourly transaction count)}.
\]

\paragraph{Trade size (from \texttt{dex\_swaps.csv}).}

Positive USD notionals $q_i = |\texttt{amount\_usd}_i|$ are fitted to
a lognormal:
\[
  \log q_i \sim \mathcal{N}(\mu_q, \sigma_q^2),
  \qquad
  \hat{\mu}_q = \overline{\log q_i},\quad
  \hat{\sigma}_q = \mathrm{sd}(\log q_i).
\]

\paragraph{Gas dynamics (from \texttt{dex\_swaps.csv}).}

Let $g_t = \texttt{gas\_price\_wei}_t / 10^9$ (gas price in Gwei).
An AR(1) is fitted to $\log g_t$:
\[
  \log g_t = \hat{\rho}\,\log g_{t-1} + \hat{\epsilon}_t,
  \qquad
  \hat{\rho} = \mathrm{corr}(\log g_t,\, \log g_{t-1}).
\]
The AR(1) residual standard deviation $\hat{\sigma}_\epsilon$ is
also stored.

\paragraph{LVR (from \texttt{dex\_lvr\_hourly.csv}).}

\[
  \overline{\texttt{lvr\_rate\_ann}},\quad
  \mathrm{median}(\texttt{lvr\_rate\_ann}),\quad
  \overline{\texttt{lvr\_usd\_tvl\_approx}},\quad
  \overline{\texttt{lvr\_to\_fee\_ratio}}.
\]

  $(p^{\mathrm{DEX}} - p^{\mathrm{CEX}}) / p^{\mathrm{CEX}} \times 10{,}000$. \\
\texttt{arbitrage\_flag} &
  $|\texttt{dex\_cex\_basis\_bps}| > 5$. \\
\texttt{net\_pnl\_usd} &
  $\texttt{burned} + \texttt{collected} - \texttt{minted}$. \\
\texttt{range\_width\_pct} &
  $100 \times (1.0001^{\Delta\texttt{tick}} - 1)$, capped at 500\%. \\
\texttt{log\_price\_change} &
  $-2(\log s_n - \log s_{n-1})$ where $s = \texttt{sqrtPriceX96}$;
  NaN for cross-block pairs. \\
\texttt{gas\_cost\_usd} &
  $\texttt{gas\_cost\_eth} \times \texttt{eth\_usdc\_price}$. \\
\texttt{sigma\_daily} &
  $\mathrm{sd}(\texttt{log\_return\_1h}) \times \sqrt{24}$. \\
\texttt{lambda\_hour} &
  $\overline{\texttt{tx\_count}}$ (mean hourly tx count). \\

\section{Known Limitations}
\label{sec:limits}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Gas cost coverage.} The \texttt{gas\_cost\_usd} column
        in \texttt{dex\_swaps.csv} is NaN for approximately 43\% of
        swaps, because The Graph does not return gas data for every block
        in the subgraph index. Aggregate gas statistics in the calibration
        file use only the 57\% of rows with positive \texttt{gas\_cost\_usd}.

  \item \textbf{LP fee income approximation.} The formula
        \texttt{fee\_income\_usd = collected $-$ burned} is approximate.
        In Uniswap v3, the \texttt{collect} call bundles accumulated
        uncollected fees with any principal tokens returned by a prior
        burn. The net figure is zero or positive when fees exceed returned
        principal, but can be negative for positions that collected fees
        between burns in unusual sequences. Treat this as an order-of-magnitude
        measure, not an exact accounting.

  \item \textbf{Subgraph USD pricing.} All \texttt{\_usd} fields in the
        raw import files are computed by the subgraph using its internal
        ETH/USD bundle price at indexing time, which may diverge from the
        CEX spot price. The DEX swap price uses the on-chain sqrtPriceX96
        (exact), but volume and fee USD estimates retain subgraph bundle
        error.

  \item \textbf{USDC depeg.} USDC briefly depegged from \$1.00 in March
        2023 (approximately 2023-03-10 to 2023-03-13). All USD fields
        during this window are measured in non-par USDC. The basis
        computation and fee-income estimates are affected accordingly.

  \item \textbf{LVR TVL approximation.} The formula $\mathrm{TVL}/8 \times
        \sigma^2$ assumes the pool is balanced (half in each token at the
        current price). In practice, as the price moves to the edges of
        the concentrated liquidity range, one token dominates TVL and the
        approximation overstates LVR. The active-liquidity formula
        (\texttt{lvr\_rate\_ann\_v3\_approx}) partially corrects for this
        but uses raw pool liquidity units that are not directly comparable
        to USD.

  \item \textbf{Position key merges NFT positions.} The LP aggregation
        uses (owner, tick\_lower, tick\_upper) as the key. When the owner
        is the NonfungiblePositionManager contract, all NFT-wrapped
        positions at the same range are pooled into a single row. This is
        acceptable for aggregate pool-level analysis but prevents per-LP
        attribution for such positions.

  \item \textbf{Annualization convention.} Realized volatility is
        annualized as $\sigma_{\mathrm{hourly}} \times \sqrt{365.25 \times
        24}$ (hourly step) or $\sigma_{\mathrm{daily}} \times
        \sqrt{365.25}$ (daily step). The two conventions are numerically
        consistent: $\sigma_{\mathrm{daily}} = \sigma_{\mathrm{hourly}}
        \times \sqrt{24}$ and the product $\sigma_{\mathrm{daily}} \times
        \sqrt{365.25} = \sigma_{\mathrm{hourly}} \times \sqrt{8760}$.
        Both give the same annualized number when applied to the correct
        return frequency.

  \item \textbf{Multi-tier pool data not processed here.} The processing
        pipeline documented in this file covers only the main 0.05\% pool
        (\texttt{data\_raw/DEX/}). The multi-tier data in
        \texttt{data\_raw/multitier/} requires separate processing scripts
        (not yet implemented) that apply the same enrichment steps to
        each fee tier.
\end{enumerate}
